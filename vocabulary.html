Vocabulary Lessons

Expand your vocabulary and express yourself more effectively!

Lesson 1: Synonyms and Antonyms

Understanding synonyms and antonyms can help you diversify your vocabulary. It's essential to learn both to express yourself with variety and precision.

Quiz: Synonyms and Antonyms

1. Which word is a synonym for "happy"?

Joyful
Sad

2. Which is the antonym of "hot"?

Warm
Cold

3. Choose the correct synonym for "fast".

Quick
Slow

4. Which of the following is an antonym for "difficult"?

Easy
Complicated

5. Which word is not a synonym for "angry"?

Furious
Joyful

Lesson 2: Homophones

Homophones are words that sound the same but have different meanings and spellings. These can be tricky but are crucial for mastering spelling and understanding context.

Quiz: Homophones

1. Which pair are homophones?

Bear / Bare
Cat / Dog

2. Choose the correct homophone: "I need ___ of those apples."

Bare
Two

3. Which homophone fits in this sentence: "He will ___ the heavy load."?

Bear
Bare

4. Choose the correct homophone for the sentence: "She will ___ the meeting."

Lead
Led

5. Which homophone completes this sentence: "Please ___ a smile for the camera."

Wear
Where

Lesson 3: Idioms

Idioms are expressions whose meanings aren't always literal, and they're often cultural. Knowing idioms can make you sound more like a native speaker and improve your understanding of a language.

Quiz: Idioms

1. What does the idiom "Break the ice" mean?

Initiate a conversation
Break something physically

2. Which idiom means "very easy"?

Piece of cake
Break the bank

3. Choose the idiom that means "to feel nervous or anxious."

Break the ice
Butterflies in the stomach

4. Which idiom means "to give up"?

Break the ice
Throw in the towel

5. What does the idiom "Hit the sack" mean?

Go to sleep
Hit something hard

Lesson 4: Prefixes and Suffixes

Prefixes and suffixes can change the meaning of a word. Understanding these can help you guess the meaning of unfamiliar words and improve your vocabulary.

Quiz: Prefixes and Suffixes

1. What does the prefix "un-" usually mean?

Not
Very

2. Which suffix turns an adjective into an adverb?

-ly
-ness

3. What is the meaning of the prefix "re-"?

Again
Before

4. Which suffix is used to form the noun of "happy"?

-ful
-ness

5. What does the prefix "mis-" usually mean?

Wrong
Right

Lesson 5: Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs are expressions made of a verb and a preposition or adverb. They often have meanings that can't be deduced from the individual words.

Quiz: Phrasal Verbs

1. What does "give up" mean?

Quit
Start

2. Choose the correct meaning for "get along".

Have a good relationship
Fight

3. Which phrasal verb means "to take care of"?

Look after
Look up

4. What does "turn down" mean?

Reject
Accept

5. Choose the correct meaning of "put off".

Postpone
Bring forward
