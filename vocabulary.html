Vocabulary Lessons

Expand your vocabulary and express yourself more effectively!

Lesson 1: Context Clues

Context clues help determine the meaning of unfamiliar words. There are five types:

  • Definition: The meaning is directly stated.
    • "An arboretum, a place where trees grow, is my favorite spot."
  • Synonym: A similar word is provided.
    • "She was ecstatic, or extremely happy, about her birthday."
  • Antonym: The opposite meaning is given.
    • "Unlike the fragile vase, the metal box was very strong."
  • Example: Examples help explain the word.
    • "Many amphibians, such as frogs and salamanders, live on land and water."
  • Inference: The meaning is implied.
    • "She is a pessimist, always expecting things to go wrong."

Lesson 2: Word Affixes (Prefixes and Suffixes)

Prefixes and suffixes change a word’s meaning:

  • Prefixes: Added at the beginning.
    • "Un- (not) → Unhappy, Re- (again) → Redo"
  • Suffixes: Added at the end.
    • "-er (person who does) → Teacher, -able (can be) → Breakable"

Lesson 3: Synonyms and Antonyms

Synonyms have similar meanings, while antonyms have opposite meanings.

  • Synonyms:
    • "Happy → Joyful, Cheerful. Big → Huge, Large."
  • Antonyms:
    • "Hot → Cold. Fast → Slow."

Lesson 4: Homonyms (Words with Multiple Meanings)

Homonyms are words with different meanings based on context:

  • Bat:
    • "A flying animal / A baseball stick."
  • Bank:
    • "A financial institution / The side of a river."
  • Light:
    • "Not heavy / Something that illuminates."

Lesson 5: Idiomatic Expressions

Idioms are phrases that don’t mean exactly what they say:

  • Break a leg:
    • "Good luck."
  • Hit the sack:
    • "Go to sleep."
  • Under the weather:
    • "Feeling sick."
