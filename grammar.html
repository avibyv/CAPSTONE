Lesson 1: Parts of Speech

Understanding parts of speech is the foundation of grammar. These are the different categories of words that we use to build sentences. Let's break them down:

  • Nouns: A noun is a word that represents a person, place, thing, or idea.

Lesson 2: Subject-Verb Agreement

Subject-verb agreement means that the subject and verb in a sentence must agree in number. If the subject is singular, the verb must be singular; if the subject is plural, the verb must be plural.

  • She runs every day. (Singular subject "She" and singular verb "runs")

Lesson 3: Tenses

Lesson 4: Conditional Sentences

Conditional sentences express situations and their possible outcomes. There are four types: zero, first, second, and third conditionals.

  • Zero Conditional: Used for facts or general truths.
    • If water boils, it turns to steam.

Lesson 5: Active and Passive Voice

The voice of a verb describes whether the subject is performing the action (active) or receiving the action (passive).

  • Active Voice: The subject performs the action.
    • The chef cooked the meal.
  • Passive Voice: The subject receives the action.
    • The meal was cooked by the chef.

Quiz: Active and Passive Voice

3. Which sentence is in passive voice?
